The high cost of commercial optomechanical components frequently restricts the accessibility of precision optics for private users. This project develops a low-cost system with kinematic mounts, utilizing a Mach-Zehnder interferometer as a benchmark for characterization. By leveraging FDM-optimized manufacturing and expanded alignment options, a universal open-source toolkit is realized, significantly reducing the barriers to entry for experimental photonics.
