The quality of microscopy immersion oil is monitored by using fluorescence increase as an indicator for aging and contamination. The setup involves coupling excitation light into a slide, guiding it via total internal reflection, and directing it into the oil through a specific angular corridor. A sharp interface edge is used to define a precise excitation volume. By comparing fluorescence signals, a threshold can be defined to identify and discard degraded or contaminated oil, thereby safeguarding optimal imaging performance.
