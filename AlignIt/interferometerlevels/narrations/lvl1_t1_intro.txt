LVL1_TASK1_INTRO - Laser Alignment Narration Script
=====================================================

[Opening tone, welcoming]
Welcome to the Michelson Interferometer simulation. This is one of the most 
important instruments in optical physics. It won Albert Michelson the first 
Nobel Prize in Physics in 1907, and today it's used in cutting-edge applications 
from gravitational wave detection to medical imaging.

[Task introduction]
Your first task is to align the laser beam along what we call the optical axis. 
Imagine an invisible line running through the center of your optical table. 
The laser beam should follow this line precisely.

[Specific instructions]
You'll see the laser positioned at [-2, -7]. The beamsplitter will eventually 
be placed at position [-2, -2]. Your job is to direct the laser beam so it 
passes exactly through that future beamsplitter position.

[How to use controls]
Use the rotation controls on all three axes - x, y, and z - to aim the beam. 
Look at where the red laser ray is traveling. Adjust until it points directly 
toward the coordinates [-2, -2].

[Why this matters]
Precision alignment from the start is crucial. Every optical component downstream 
depends on having the beam come from the right direction. This is true in any 
optical system you'll ever build.

[Completion expectation]
When you're done, the beam should travel straight along the optical axis, ready 
for the beamsplitter to be installed. Take your time - accuracy now prevents 
frustration later.
