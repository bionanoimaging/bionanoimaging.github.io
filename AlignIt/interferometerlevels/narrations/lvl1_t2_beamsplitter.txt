LVL1_TASK2_BEAMSPLITTER - Beamsplitter Installation Narration Script
=====================================================================

[Context setting]
Now we install the heart of the interferometer: the beamsplitter. This optical 
component is what makes the Michelson design so elegant and powerful.

[What a beamsplitter does]
A beamsplitter is a partially reflective mirror - typically coated to reflect 
50% of incoming light while transmitting the other 50%. In our simulation, 
when your laser beam hits it at 45 degrees, you'll see two beams emerge: 
one transmitted straight through, and one reflected at a right angle.

[Placement instructions]
Position the beamsplitter at coordinates [-2, -2], exactly where your laser 
beam is traveling. The rotation should be 45 degrees relative to the incoming 
beam. This orientation ensures that half the light goes toward what will be 
Mirror 1, and half toward Mirror 2.

[Verification step]
You can verify correct alignment when both output beams are clearly visible 
and have approximately equal brightness. If one beam is much dimmer than the 
other, check that your beamsplitter is truly at 45 degrees.

[Why this matters]
The beamsplitter serves double duty: it splits the light on the way out, and 
it recombines the light on the way back. This is why precise positioning is 
critical - any error here propagates through the entire system.

[Looking ahead]
Once the beamsplitter is in place, we'll add the two mirrors at the ends of 
each arm. Each mirror will send its beam back to the beamsplitter, where they 
recombine and create the interference pattern that reveals path length differences.
